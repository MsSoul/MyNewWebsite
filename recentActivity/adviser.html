GRADE 12 STEM RESEARCH ADVISER: MISTIFY SYSTEM

Final Defense: Feb. 23, 2026 - Holy Trinity College
Image Source: https://www.facebook.com/photo.php?fbid=1535945961868401&set=pb.100063590364810.-2207520000&type=3

I am incredibly proud and honored to have served as the research adviser for a brilliant group of Grade 12 STEM students who successfully hurdled their final research defense on February 23, 2026. Demonstrating excellent scientific rigor and innovative thinking, my advisees were officially nominated for the prestigious Best Research Award.

Their breakthrough study, titled “MISTIFY: THE EFFECTIVENESS OF AN ARDUINO MEGA-BASED SYSTEM IN DETECTING TO REDUCE INDOOR AIR POLLUTANTS THROUGH AUTOMATED MISTING,” focused on developing a smart, closed-loop automation platform to improve indoor air quality. Driven by an Arduino Mega microcontroller, the prototype integrates a comprehensive sensor array, including a PMS5003 particulate matter sensor, an MH-Z19B carbon dioxide sensor, and a DHT22 temperature sensor. When environmental parameters breach safe thresholds or the temperature reaches or exceeds 35°C, the system triggers a localized mitigation mechanism: an ultrasonic mist transducer that disperses a fine mist of 1% citric acid mixed with 99% distilled water, paired with dual DC-powered dispersion fans and a carbon filter to trap particles and safely neutralize gases.

As their research adviser, I guided them closely through every phase of this intensive study, ensuring they had the necessary resources and technical direction to bring their engineering concepts to life. I worked with them meticulously on structural revisions, providing hands-on assistance to clean up and professionally format their manuscript lines, data tables, and overall documentation. Throughout the development process, I actively brainstormed with the team, proposing aligned engineering adjustments and structural solutions that kept their automation objectives sharp and practical. Seeing their dedication translate into flawless prototype execution and a major research nomination was a deeply rewarding experience.
